Terms and Privacy for Openly

Rules

Openly is an open community of college students who are sharing their most heartfelt thoughts with their fellow classmates. Everything you post is shared anonymously across your school’s community and there is no way for others to identify you. Your .edu email serves as a key, which allows you to sail into the Openly. Here are some guidelines to adhere to for Openly:

  1. We have a zero tolerance for bullying.
  2. You cannot post an individual's name when used in a negative context.
  3. You cannot post personal identifiers such as phone number, email, address, or any social media accounts.
  4. You cannot spam/pollute the messaging feed with pointless and repetitive posts.
  5. You cannot impersonate other members of the community.

 

Privacy

Here at Openly, we believe that your privacy is our top priority and we take every measure to ensure that your identity remains private. We built our application on a secure backend, which lessens the possibility of any security breach. By eliminating the use of usernames, passwords, and photos we’re able to prevent the likelihood that another user will identify you. The only way another user can identify you is if you personal decide to reveal your identity. We only ask for your .edu email and your gender in order to place you in the same category as your classmates, as well as to let other users know if they’re talking to a girl or a guy. You wouldn’t want a 14-year-old male posting crap about your school and pretending to be a girl, would you? The information we collect eliminates this threat entirely.
